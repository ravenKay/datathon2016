Datathon 2016 - Governor's Workforce Innovation

What are the jobs trends and emerging sectors in my hometown? Do I have the skill set and education to fill these emerging opportunities? What are the up and coming sectors or areas in the new Virginia economy?

Explore the below graphics for a visual look at the current and future state of the new Virginia economy. Compare the availability of Virginia jobs by location, skill set, and education. Discover the booming sectors in the Virginia economy: Technology/Consulting, Health and Finance. Observe the geographic regions in search of talented human resources.

Solution/Value Proposition:The solution focused on providing interactive visuals to display the job industries and skill sets that are currently in demand and trending in the Virginia economy. While there is a large amount of data on Virginia's job market openings, there is a need for clear, concise information in order to provide consumers with actionable insight in their decision making.

Discovery and Analysis: The last five years of data indicate that the booming sectors of the new Virginia economy include Technology/Consulting, Health, Finance, Food/Restaurant and the Retail Industries. While the clear trend is shifting towards STEM fields such as technology or engineering, demand remains consistent for other skill sets such customer service and sales focused positions. Job availability trends concentrate in the North and Northeastern portions of Virginia. In regards to education, Associate and Bachelors degree earners may earn their education in more geographically diverse areas than just the Northeast. While many do earn their degrees in the Northeastern cities of Virginia such as Arlington and Fairfax, Richmond, Virginia Beach, and Norfolk produce several higher education graduates as well.

Technical Execution: Technical decisions included utilizing bootstrap for a quick front end application solution and Tableau for data analysis and visualization. Future technical needs are to connect the data to be real time to allow more up to date and accurate information.

Data Sources: Job Postings 2010 -- 2015 (Part 1), Job Postings 2010 -- 2015 (Part 3) and Virginia Job Postings 2016 (Shared by VirginiaOpenData), Higher Education (Shared by asmith_VEDP).

Data Set Reasoning: Based on the human capital supply and demand focus in the above visuals, selection was based on strong data sources for qualifications, location, and sector observations.

Customer Segment and Validation: The team conducted interviews with a handle of current job seekers, with a particular focus on those selecting which industry or skill set to focus on as they approach a graduation date.
